Business Support & Technical Assistance

Interested in starting your business in downtown Augusta? We’re here to help!

  1. Home
  2. For Businesses

Connect to the Downtown alliance and build your customer base.

Joining the Augusta Downtown Alliance puts your business in front of the people who actually walk, shop, dine, and spend money downtown. When you connect with the Alliance, you tap into coordinated marketing, community events that drive real foot traffic, and a network of local partners who actively promote each other. If you want more visibility, stronger customer relationships, and a voice in the growth of downtown Augusta, this is where you start.

How to Apply

1

Submit Application

Send us your completed application to begin the onboarding process. The sooner it’s in, the sooner we can start promoting your business.

2

Provide any required supporting documents so we can verify details and move your application forward without delays.

You’ll get a clear decision with next steps. If approved, we’ll immediately begin integrating your business into Alliance promotions and programs.

Historic Preservation Tax Credits

In 2017, the Water Street Historic District was added to the National Register of Historic Places, making redevelopment of qualifying buildings within the historic district eligible for available federal and state historic preservation tax credits. For more information or to see if your property is eligible, contact the Maine State Historic Preservation Office.

■ Historic Preservation Grants

Water Street’s designation as a National Register Historic District also makes certain properties eligible for historic preservation related grant opportunities, summaries of which are available at this link. If you are interested in pursuing one of these grants, the Augusta Downtown Alliance may be able to partner with you! Please contact our office for more information.

Business Sign Grants

To celebrate Water Street’s designation as a National Register Historic District, and to highlight a more historically-inspired street, the Augusta Downtown Alliance and the City of Augusta partnered to establish a Business Sign Grant Program to assist new and existing business owners with obtaining new signage.

A business that meets program eligibility requirements can apply for up to $750 to go towards new signage. To initiate the application process, to ask questions, or to obtain assistance in completing this application, please visit the City of Augusta’s website, Linked Here.

■ Downtown TIF & Credit Enhancement Agreements

Tax Increment Financing, or TIF, is a financial mechanism that municipalities use to stimulate economic development. By designating a specific geographic area as a TIF district, the City of Augusta can channel the increased property tax revenues generated by new development within this area to fund public infrastructure projects or support private development through Credit Enhancement Agreements (CEAs). A Credit Enhancement Agreement is a legal agreement between the City and a developer or business. It is a mechanism to assist the development project by using all, or a percentage of, the tax revenues generated by the new development to pay certain authorized project costs. Payments are made directly to the developer or business.

The majority of Downtown Augusta is within the City’s Downtown TIF geographic boundaries, meaning that your development or redevelopment project may be eligible for a Credit Enhancement Agreement. These are typically reserved for large-scale redevelopment projects that bring in a significant amount of jobs or investment. For more information, we encourage you to contact the City’s Economic Development Bureau at (207) 626-2365.
